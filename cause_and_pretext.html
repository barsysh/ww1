The origins of the First World War are deeply rooted in the political and imperial dynamics of the early 20th century. Tensions among European powers were escalating due to territorial disputes, nationalistic ambitions, and an intricate system of alliances. The immediate pretext for the war was the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary by a Serbian nationalist. This event triggered a series of diplomatic crises and mobilizations, eventually leading to declarations of war. The alliances, including the Triple Entente and the Triple Alliance, played a crucial role in transforming a regional conflict into a global war.
